 Relational database modeling process – normalization 
 Objective: Reduce data redundancy and improve data integrity 
 
 Organizing the columns (attributes) and tables (relations) of a database to 
ensure that their dependencies are properly enforced by database integrity 
constraints. 
 
 Relations are progressively decomposed into smaller relations to a point 
where all attributes in a relation are very tightly coupled with the primary 
key of the relation.

--- PAGE 3 ---
 First normal form (1NF):  
 Data items are atomic 
 Rows are unique 
 Second normal form (2NF): No "partial" dependencies (depends on the whole key) 
 Single column primary key. (All non-prime attributes (attributes that are not 
part of a candidate key) must be fully functionally dependent on the entire 
primary key, not just part of it.) 
 Third normal form (3NF): No "transitive" dependencies (depends only on the key) 
 all non-key attributes are completely independent of each other. (A non-
prime attribute (an attribute not part of any candidate key) must depend only 
on the primary key and not on another non-prime attribute.)

Relation Database Theory, cont’d 
 The process of normalization generally breaks a table into many tables where 
attributes depends on primary keys. 
 A normalized database yields a flexible model, making it easy to maintain 
dynamic relationships between business entities. 
 Normalized tables are effective and efficient for operational databases – a lot 
of inserts and updates (aiming at optimizing insert and update speed).  
 
 However, normalized tables are not friendly to analysis. Many tables need to 
be joined to conduct analysis.
