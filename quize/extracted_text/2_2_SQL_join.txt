Join 
• Most databases have many tables 
• Related data are stored in different tables 
• SQL JOIN is used to combine rows from two or more tables based on related column(s) 
between them.  
• It’s one of the core concepts in relational databases.  
 
• Specify join condition 
– Can be any comparison but usually = 
– PK = FK most common join condition 
– ER diagram useful when combining tables

Specifying Join Condition Using JOIN in FROM 
Clause 
• Use INNER JOIN and ON keywords 
• FROM clause contains join operations 
SELECT OfferNo, CourseNo, FacFirstName, FacLastName   
 FROM Offering INNER JOIN Faculty  
   ON Faculty.FacNo = Offering.FacNo 
 WHERE OffTerm = 'FALL' AND OffYear = 2016  
   AND FacRank = 'ASST' AND CourseNo LIKE 'IS%';

Specifying Join Condition in WHERE Clause 
• List tables in the FROM clause 
• List join conditions in the WHERE clause 
SELECT OfferNo, CourseNo, FacFirstName, 
FacLastName  
 FROM Offering, Faculty  
 WHERE OffTerm = 'FALL' AND OffYear = 2016  
   AND FacRank = 'ASST' AND CourseNo LIKE 'IS%' 
   AND Faculty.FacNo = Offering.FacNo;

--- PAGE 7 ---
Join More than two Tables 
Example: List Leonard Vince’s teaching schedule in fall 2016.  For each course, list the 
offering number, course number, number of units, days, location, and time.  
SELECT OfferNo, Offering.CourseNo, OffDays, 
       CrsUnits, OffLocation, OffTime 
 FROM Offering INNER JOIN Course  
   ON Offering.CourseNo = Course.CourseNo 
   INNER JOIN Faculty ON Offering.FacNo = Faculty.FacNo 
 WHERE OffYear = 2016 AND OffTerm = 'FALL'   
   AND FacFirstName = 'LEONARD'  
   AND FacLastName = 'VINCE';
